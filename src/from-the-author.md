# From the Author

I am writing this book to understand `rustc` so I can contribute to it. If it ends up useful to you, that is a second purpose served, not the first.

A few honest notes before you start.

## Expect errors, please open PRs

The book is verified against `rustc 1.95.0`, but `rustc` evolves and I am still learning. Some passages will turn out to be wrong, others to drift as the codebase moves. If you have experience with what a chapter discusses and you spot something off, please open a PR against the book's repo. I will be happy to learn from it, and the next reader will benefit.

## "How I'd like to be taught rustc"

I wrote each chapter with the framing of how I would have wanted this material introduced to me on my way in. Some of the scaffolding (the "What you'll learn" abstracts, the recall quizzes, the per-lab "What you should see" reveals) helped *me* keep the concepts. If they feel redundant to you, fly past them. The book's argument will still hold.

## On co-authorship with AI

This book was co-authored with Claude, the model made by Anthropic. What that means in practice:

- **The architecture and editorial judgement are mine**: the chapter ordering, the four-section rhythm, the lab design, the verify-claims discipline, the voice spec, and every decision about what to include and what to cut.
- **Claude did the heavy lifting on draft generation, structural editing, and the per-chapter verification fanouts** that cross-checked every named function, type, and line number against `rustc 1.95.0` in a local rustc clone.
- **I reviewed every change.** The book's accuracy and shape are not delegated; they are mine to own.

I am stating this up front because readers deserve to know how the tool was used, and because I would rather build trust on disclosure than risk it on omission. Every claim about specific `rustc` files, functions, or behavior was cross-checked against the actual source at the pinned commit before being shipped.

## On expertise

I am not a compiler expert. Maybe one day. I keep an open mind to learning and to changing my opinions when evidence calls for it. If something is wrong, please be kind and direct when you correct me. I will do the same.

## A sibling project

I also maintain **rustc Atlas** ([github.com/gme-muriuki/atlas](https://github.com/gme-muriuki/atlas)), an interactive map of the Rust compiler. It indexes the `rust-lang/rust` workspace and draws it as a clickable graph: each crate is a box, lines show which crates depend on which, click any box to open the module tree inside. If this book is your guide to *what* the compiler does, the atlas helps you find *where* it does it. Useful as a companion while you read.
